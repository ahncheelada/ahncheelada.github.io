GroupRead

M.A. Graduate Thesis 2011

GroupRead is a collaborative reading platform that focuses on higher order reading comprehension for middle school learners. While observing 6th-8th grade classrooms, my partner, Isabel Cheng, and I saw the pedagogical importance of peer discussions in developing a deeper and critical understanding of texts.

However, once students left the classroom to complete reading assignments, there were few opportunities to continue this dialogue and even less scaffolding on how to respectfully and thoughtfully engage with peers about reading.

GroupRead leverages the affordances of online and social communication to encourage conversations around reading and create a community amongst young readers.

The goal is to create a space where readers can connect and, by building off each other, develop a personal and constructive comprehension of the text.

User Research

We did several rounds of user testing, beginning first with paper prototypes to introduce the concept and test the interface.

We then did a analogous experience test utilizing Google Docs to observe how learners might interact in a similar commenting environment.

This test validated that the side-by-side text and commenting does encourage and enable students to discuss and interact with one another about the reading.

Students asked questions and responded to each other with their own thoughts. They were humorous and casually at ease with one another.

Design Concepts

Our designs went through several iterations. We focussed on providing different ways of scaffolding conversation, including polling, questions, and creating connections.

We also considered small "missions" that students could challenge one another to engage with the content and utilize game models.

Though the project ended with our graduate work, Isabel and I still see future opportunities with GroupRead to continue to experiment with the dialogue interactions and create a space where students can safely learn how to have productive critical conversation. We'd also love to further design what the teacher role could look like within the platform and how the data collected on student activity could be a formative assessment tool.

iClicker REEF Onboarding

iClicker had already established a presence in college classrooms as an interactive polling solution to help teachers formatively assess student understanding with handheld remotes. However, their new mobile, app-based product, REEF, was having a difficult start.

The complexity of their product ecosystem and the resistance to change in larger academic institutions was proving a challenging transition from analog to digital.

Strategy

As the lead designer of the project with Theresa Neil Strategy & Design, I began with stakeholder and customer support interviews. We identified the sources of teacher and administrative troubleshooting.

Collaborating with client leadership in multi-day workshops, we mapped priorities and generated potential solutions. We then revisited the strategic models of the experience and overhauled the on-boarding flow.

 

Short-Term

As a short-term solution for the approaching semester, we created and simplified educational collateral to help students help themselves and equip teachers with tools to scaffold student autonomy.

 

Iterative Design Research

Following over a series of months, we interviewed, designed, tested, and iterated the on-boarding experiences with 3 main user groups: students, teachers, and school administrators.

For students, the goal was to streamline the course joining process and to clarify the app’s relationship with physical Clickers and payment structure.

For teachers, the design was all about communication, managing course invitations, and LMS integration.

School administrators needed the ability to help teachers but also to monitor usage and ensure the value of purchasing REEF for their school.

At the end of the 10-month project engagement, we handed off strategic wireframes for the internal design team to continue into development.
